Academic Integrity

Resources & Citations

This project is built upon rigorous research. The following sources were essential in uncovering and validating the erased histories presented across this website. There are also resources I used to help make the website.

Digital Archives & Institutional Websites

  • IEEE Spectrum. "Granville T. Woods: The 'Black Edison' Who Invented the Train Telegraph."

    Details on Woods's many patents, including the induction telegraph for trains.

  • U.S. Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO). "Bringing Light to All: Lewis Latimer."

    Information on Latimer's contributions to the carbon filament for the lightbulb.

  • NASA Landsat. "A Face Behind Landsat Images: Meet Dr. Valerie L. Thomas."

    Biography focusing on Thomas’s work developing the illusion transmitter technology.

  • Lemelson-MIT Program. "Mark Dean and Dennis Moeller."

    Details on Dean’s role in the development of the IBM PC and the ISA bus.

  • The Henry Ford. "Jerry Lawson and the Fairchild Channel F Video Entertainment System."

    Information on Lawson’s pioneering work on removable video game cartridges.

  • Greenbelt Museum. "Sarah Boone’s Modern Ironing Board."

    Details on Boone’s 1892 patent for the improved, reversible ironing board.

  • Tasting Table. "The Man Who Invented The Bread Machine In The 1800s."

    The story of Joseph Lee, who invented the bread kneading machine and patented the automatic dough kneader.

  • History.com. "Was the Potato Chip Invented in Saratoga, New York?"

    Context on George Crum and the accidental invention of the potato chip.

  • New Georgia Encyclopedia. "Alonzo Herndon (1858-1927)."

    Biography of Alonzo Herndon, a former slave who became a successful entrepreneur and founder of Atlanta Life Insurance Company.

  • Jim Crow Museum, Ferris State University. "What was the Green Book?"

    Information on Victor Green's guide for safe travel for African Americans during the Jim Crow era.

  • U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA). "Continuing César Chávez's Legacy Supporting Farmworkers."

    Article on Chávez's impact on agricultural labor rights and social movements.

  • National Women's History Museum. "Dolores Huerta."

    Biography of the labor leader and civil rights activist who co-founded the UFW.

  • PRRAC — Connecting Research to Advocacy. "Censored, Erased, and Whitewashed: Jim Crow Education in the Twenty-First Century." P & R Article (January-April 2024).

    A key source analyzing the ongoing effects of historical erasure within education systems.

  • American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU). "The Movement to Erase Black History, Culture, and Joy."

    Podcast episode discussing the current political and educational efforts to censor Black history.

  • TIME Magazine. "The Black Panther Party's Forgotten History of Fighting Health Care Inequality."

    Details on the public health and medical initiatives run by the BPP.

  • National Women's History Museum. "Alice Ball."

    Biography and details regarding the theft and subsequent restoration of Alice Ball's work.

  • Smithsonian Magazine. "The Nation’s First Black Female Doctor Blazed a Path..."

    Article detailing the life and historical erasure of Dr. Rebecca Lee Crumpler.

  • Medical News Today. "The stolen cells of Henrietta Lacks and their ongoing contribution to science."

    Information on the HeLa cell line and the ethical issues surrounding its use without consent.

  • Frontiers in Science. "Children in Science: Jane C. Wright – A Forgotten Pioneer..."

    Details on Dr. Jane Cooke Wright’s pioneering work in cancer chemotherapy.

  • National Women's History Museum. "Edmonia Lewis (c. 1844–1907)."

    Biography of the celebrated Black/Indigenous sculptor, Edmonia Lewis.

  • Wikimedia Commons. "File:Edmonia Lewis by Henry Rocher.jpg."

    Source for portrait photography of Edmonia Lewis.

  • African American Intellectual History Society (AAIHS). "Zora Neale Hurston's Anthropological Legacy."

    Analysis of Hurston's pioneering work in anthropology and folklore collection.

  • KPBS. "Early Novel Written By Free Black Woman Called Out Racism Among Abolitionists."

    Focus on Julia C. Collins's early work and the context of its disappearance.

  • PBS American Experience. "Bessie Coleman."

    Biography of the first African American and Native American woman pilot.

  • Columbia School of International and Public Affairs (SIG). "Black History Month, Part I: Historic Contributions..."

    Highlights the historical context and challenges faced by Black innovators.

  • Learn HTML. "Learning By examples"

    Shows the basics of code so you the coder can make great work of your own.

  • Learn HTML. "HyperText Markup Language"

    The articles below can help you learn more about HTML.
